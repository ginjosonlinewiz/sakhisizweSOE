About Us

In December 2015 we started work towards a school soccer development program with the assistance of CALUSAâ€™s Cala Youth Commune (CYC). The main goal is to help aspiring school soccer players in Cala to realize their dreams of becoming professional soccer players. This goal will be achieved by developing the players at their schools then finding them professional teams to play for after matric.

Originally South African National First Division (NFD) team FC Cape Town was the main professional team that the players from the sports development plan were going to feed into as per agreement I reached with them late 2015. In fact 17 year old striker Kuhle Ndletyana from Batandwa Ndondo Senior Secondary School came to Cape Town to be seen by FC Cape Town in January 2016. They liked him. When he is done with matric at the end of this year he is supposed to have another trial, this time for a permanent deal.

However, upon hearing about Elliot United FC and then seeing the team play in February I felt that it would be better for Kuhle Ndletyana to try out at Elliot United rather instead of FC Cape Town. Elliot United play in the ABC Motsepe league (one division below the NFD) and with the set up Elliot United has, Kuhle will still develop and improve further to become very good striker at the highest level.

Kuhle is just one of many talented players in the school development program. The schools in the program are from all over the Sakhisizwe municipal district. Currently there are 6 schools in the program with a total of 76 under-19 players in the data base. From what I have seen so far, the Sakhisizwe players are more talented than the players I have seen in Cape Town. With the right timing and with the right management these players could easily compete in the PSL. And I believe it would make more sense that they put their talent to use for teams closer to home, with Elliot United currently being the team of choice. This is because Elliot United play at the highest level out of all the local teams in Sakhisizwe.

With the above scenario in mind, Cala Youth Commune (CYC) is in a very good position to establish a CYC Soccer School of Excellence. The CYC School of Excellence will accommodate 18 of the most promising players in the school soccer development program. The players will go on a specialized training program designed to accelerate their development.
